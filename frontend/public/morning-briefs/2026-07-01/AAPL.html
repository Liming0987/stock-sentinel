News & Catalyst
NEUTRAL Apple in talks to buy memory chips from Pentagon-blacklisted CXMT and YMTC; CEO Tim Cook lobbying Trump administration for regulatory clearance

Bloomberg reported that Apple is negotiating to buy DRAM from ChangXin Memory Technologies (CXMT) and NAND from Yangtze Memory Technologies (YMTC) — both on the Pentagon's Section 1260H military-linked list. The motivation is straightforward: DRAM contract prices surged 55-60% in early 2026, forcing Apple to raise MacBook/iPad/Vision Pro prices by up to 20%. By sourcing from CXMT and YMTC (which already offer high-quality chips at competitive prices), Apple could reduce component costs significantly. The regulatory risk is nuanced: the 1260H designation doesn't legally bar private transactions, but Apple fears the suppliers could be escalated to the Commerce Department Entity List (a trade ban) after signing multi-billion contracts. Tim Cook has personally lobbied Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent. This creates a complex risk/reward: success = margin recovery for Apple products; failure (Entity List escalation) = supply chain disruption and political backlash.
